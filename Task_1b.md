### 1b) 

We proceed by rewriting the second-order ODE from part 1a) into two first-order ODEs for

$$\vec{y}(x) = \begin{bmatrix}
y(x) \\
y'(x)
\end{bmatrix}.$$ 

We get

$$
\frac{d\vec{y}}{dx} = \begin{bmatrix}
y'(x) \\
-4\cdot \sin(2x)
\end{bmatrix}, \quad 

\vec{y}(0) = \begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
2
\end{bmatrix}.
$$
